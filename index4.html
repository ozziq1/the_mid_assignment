These are some of the many forests and nature reserves in Indonesia:

Hutan Hujan Borneo

The Borneo rainforest is a tropical rainforest that spans three countries on the island of Borneo, namely Indonesia, Malaysia and Brunei Darussalam: Extent The Borneo rainforest covers an area of approximately 427,500 square kilometers. Age The Borneo rainforest is estimated to be around 140 million years old, making it one of the oldest forests in the world.

Hutan Papua

Papua's forests are tropical rainforests with natural beauty and rich biodiversity. These forests play an important role in preventing climate change. Here are some facts about Papua's forests: Papua's forest area is estimated at 42.5 million hectares. Papua Province has the largest protected forest area in Indonesia, covering 7.8 million hectares in 2019. West Papua has the largest mangrove forest area, covering 482,029 hectares. Papua also has 5.2 million hectares of sago fields. Papua's forests face a high threat of deforestation. Since 2000, nearly one million hectares of forest in Papua have been released for oil palm plantations. Greenpeace found that most companies in Papua were found to have committed violations.

Baluran

Baluran National Park is a nature conservation area in Situbondo Regency, East Java. Baluran National Park has an area of about 25,000 hectares. Baluran National Park has a variety of flora and fauna, including 26 species of mammals. Baluran National Park has various types of forests, such as mangrove forests, seasonal forests, coastal forests, lower mountain forests, swamp forests, and evergreen forests throughout the year. Baluran National Park has the largest savanna area on the island of Java. Baluran National Park has a variety of regional specialty plants, such as widoro bukol, neem, and pilang. Baluran National Park has Bama Beach which is famous for its clear water, white sand, and mangrove plants. Baluran National Park has a Core Zone that serves as a habitat protection for iconic Baluran National Park animals, such as banteng, Timor deer, antelope, leopard, coyote, and pangolin. Baluran National Park is nicknamed "Africa van Java" (Africa on Java Island).

Hutan Pinus Mangunan

Mangunan Pine Forest is located in Sukorame, Mangunan, Dlingo, Bantul, Yogyakarta Special Region. This forest used to be a barren area, but is managed for reforestation by planting pine, acacia, and mahogany trees. Mangunan Pine Forest is a protected forest that was opened to the public in February 2017. Mangunan Pine Forest covers an area of 573.7 hectares, with a tourist area of almost 30 hectares.

Hutan Pinus
Kragilan

Kragilan Pine Forest is a pine forest located in Magelang, Central Java. Located on the western slope of Mount Merbabu, it presents a very instagramable natural panorama. Its beauty can be seen from the lined pine trees that collaborate with the beauty of the hill beside it. With a background of rows of pine trees, it will definitely make those of you who visit become enthusiastic about taking selfies. As the name implies, Top Selfie Pinus Kragilan is indeed a place intended for those who want to take selfies. This destination is relatively new, but it has attracted the attention of both local and out-of-town tourists. Not only do they enjoy the beauty of nature, many of them are hunting photos and uploading them to social media. Apart from weekdays, the number of visitors who come here is also quite large during Ramadan, especially in the afternoon.

Gunung Pancar

Gunung Pancar Pine Forest is a natural tourist area featuring rows of towering pine trees, creating a cool and calm atmosphere in the middle of the mountains. Located in Sentul, Bogor, it offers beautiful scenery and fresh air that makes it ideal for camping, taking pictures, or simply relaxing while enjoying the beauty of nature. With its soothing atmosphere and distinctive pine scent, Gunung Pancar Pine Forest is a favorite destination for those seeking an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Limpakuwus

Limpakuwus Pine Forest, located on the slopes of Mount Slamet, Banyumas, is a natural tourist area with rows of towering pine trees, creating a quiet and cool atmosphere. The fresh mountain air combined with the beautiful scenery makes it a great place to take pictures, relax, or explore nature. Surrounded by a light mist in the morning and the distinctive aroma of pine forests, Limpakuwus offers tranquility for visitors who want to enjoy nature in a beautiful and natural setting.

Cagar Alam Pananjung

Pananjung Nature Reserve is located in the Pangandaran region of West Java and is a conservation area rich in biodiversity. The reserve is home to a variety of rare flora and fauna, such as banteng, deer and long-tailed monkeys, as well as dense tropical trees and natural caves. In addition to the lush forests, Pananjung Nature Reserve also offers beautiful beaches and coral cliffs that add to its appeal, making it an ideal place for nature lovers who want to explore the beauty and uniqueness of tropical ecosystems.

Gunung Tangkoko Batuangus

Mount Tangkoko Batuangus Nature Reserve, located in North Sulawesi, is a nature conservation area known for its biodiversity and stunning natural scenery. Here, visitors can find rare species such as tarsiers, Sulawesi black monkeys and bear cuscuses. The dense tropical forest, large trees, and distinctive volcanic rock formations add to the natural charm of this place. This nature reserve is a paradise for nature lovers and researchers who want to enjoy the natural atmosphere while witnessing the uniqueness of Sulawesi's endemic fauna in their natural habitat.

Hutan Mangrove Tarakan

Tarakan Mangrove Forest is a conservation area located in Tarakan, North Kalimantan, that offers a unique view of the lush mangrove ecosystem. It is home to a variety of species, such as mangrove crabs, proboscis monkeys, birds and small fish that play an important role in the balance of the coastal ecosystem. With a boardwalk that cuts through the forest, visitors can explore and enjoy the natural beauty while learning about the importance of mangroves as shoreline protectors and marine life buffers. The beautiful and calm atmosphere makes Tarakan Mangrove Forest a favorite destination for nature lovers.
