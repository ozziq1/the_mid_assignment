These are some of the unique villages in Indonesia:

Kampung Inggris Pare

Kampung Inggris Pare (English: Pare English Village) is a nickname for a village located around Jalan Anyelir, Jalan Brawijaya, Jalan Kemuning in Tulungrejo Village and Pelem Village, Pare District, Kediri Regency, East Java. The neighborhood has developed into a place to learn English. As a place of learning, the daily scenery in Kampung Inggris Pare is like a student complex. Dominated by the hustle and bustle of the activities of people who are studying, especially during the holiday season, this village is no less crowded than tourist attractions because of the large number of students, college students, workers and the general public who fill their vacation time to study while traveling.People who study in English Village are not only from Indonesia but also from foreign countries such as Malaysia, Thailand, Timor Leste, and others.

Kampung 3D Malang

The village located on Jalan Temenggungan Ledok, Kesatrian Village, Blimbing Subdistrict, Malang City, which used to be impressed as a slum village because it was located under the Brantas River bridge, is now slowly but surely with the touch of the local youth organization has been transformed into a 3-Dimensional Village. What is the description of the tridi village, which is located north of the Jodipan Colorful Village? What is certain is that this village presents a variety of three-dimensional characters starting from the gate to enter the village.

Kampung Baduy
Banten

Baduy Village is a traditional tourism village located in Kanekes Village, Leuwidamar District, Lebak Regency, Banten. This village is known as the residence of the Baduy Tribe, one of the indigenous Indonesian tribes that still maintains its local customs and wisdom very strongly. The Baduy tribe is divided into three groups, namely Inner Baduy, Outer Baduy, and Dangka. Each group has its own characteristics and rules in living their daily lives.

Kampung Cyber Yogyakarta

Cyber Village or RT 36 Taman Kampung is one of the villages designed by the local government of Yogyakarta Special Region (DIY) together with local residents as a tourist attraction. Cyber Village was established in 2006, by Antonius Sasongko, familiarly called Koko or Mas Sas. Cyber Village is the only tourist village in the region that is quite popular. Just imagine, this village with no more than 100 families has been visited by the founder (CEO) of Facebook, Mark Zuckerberg. In fact, it is the only one to receive an award from a soft drink manufacturer, AJE Indonesia, with a Rp100 million grant for internet infrastructure provision.

Kampung Teletubbies
Yogyakarta

Teletubbies Village is one of the favorite tourist villages in Sleman, Yogyakarta. What makes it interesting is that the rows of houses here resemble Eskimo dwellings in the Polar region but with a colorful version.Although these domed houses spoil the eyes of visitors, this village has a long and moving history behind it. In addition to resembling Eskimo houses in the polar region, the houses in this village are also nicknamed like the semicircular houses in the TV series "Teletubbies". Therefore, this village is better known as Teletubbies Village.

Kampung Trunyan Bali

Trunyan Village is a village in Kintamani District, Bangli, Bali, which has a unique funeral tradition. People who die are not buried or cremated, but only placed under the Taru Menyan tree. This tree will be able to eliminate the smell of the corpse that is there.

Kampung Kasur Pasir
Madura

Villagers in Sumenep, Madura, have a unique habit of sleeping on the sand. This unique tradition has been passed down from generation to generation by the residents of East Legung village, Sumenep district, Madura. Not only sleeping on the sand, even in front of the house there is also a kind of special terrace that is based on sand. This tradition has been a local wisdom adopted and passed down by local residents for hundreds of years. They believe that sleeping on the sand has many health benefits. So that local wisdom is still preserved until now.
