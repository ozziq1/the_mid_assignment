Komodo

Komodo dragons, or Komodo monitor lizards in full, are a species of large monitor lizard found on Komodo, Rinca, Flores, Gili Motang, and Gili Dasami islands in East Nusa Tenggara province, Indonesia. This animal is also referred to by the natives of Komodo Island by the local name ora. Speed: 20 km/h (Maximum, On land, Sprint) Scientific name: Varanus komodoensis Mass: 79 - 91 kg (Male, Adult), 68 - 73 kg (Female, Adult) Length: 2.6 m (Male, Adult), 2.3 m (Female, Adult) Kingdom: Animalia Family: Varanidae Domain: Eukaryota

Harimau Sumatera

The Sumatran tiger is the population of Panthera tigris sondaica that inhabits the Indonesian island of Sumatra and the only surviving member of the Sunda tiger subspecies. Badak Sumatera

The Sumatran rhinoceros, also known as the hairy rhinoceros or two-horned Asian rhinoceros, is an endangered species of the Rhinocerotidae family and one of the five extant rhino species. It is the only remaining species of the genus Dicerorhinus. Scientific name: Dicerorhinus sumatrensis Genus: Dicerorhinus; Gloger, 1841 Kingdom: Animalia Order: Perissodactyla Family: Rhinocerotidae Phylum: Chordata Class: Mammalia

Orangutan

Orangutans or mawas are great apes native to the rainforests of Indonesia and Malaysia. Today they are found only in parts of Borneo and Sumatra, but during the Pleistocene era they were distributed throughout Southeast Asia and southern China. Mass: 37 kg (female, adult) Scientific name: Pongo Kingdom: Animalia Gestation period: 274 days Family: Hominidae Height: 1.3 m (Female, Adult)

Gajah Kalimantan

The Borneo elephant is a subspecies of the Asian elephant and can be found in North Borneo and Sabah. The origin of this elephant, referred to as the grandmother or Gadingan by the Agabag 1 tribe, is still controversial. Scientific name: Elephas maximus borneensis Kingdom: Animalia Class: Mammalia Phylum: Chordata Genus: Elephas Order: Proboscidea Species: E. maximus

Jalak Bali

TheBali Starling ( Leucopsar rothschildi) is a medium-sized songbird, approximately 25 cm long, from the Sturnidae tribe. It is also known as the Curik rather than the Jalak. The Bali Starling is found only in the western forests of Bali Island and is endemic to Indonesia. It is also the only species endemic to Bali and in 1991 was named the faunal emblem of Bali Province.

The Javan rhino is one of the smallest rhino species, along with the Sumatran rhino, or “woolly” rhino. Penyu Hijau

Green sea turtles are large sea turtles that belong to the family Cheloniidae. They are the only species in the Chelonia family. They live in all tropical and subtropical seas, especially in the Atlantic Ocean and Pacific Ocean. Conservation status: Threatened (Declining) Encyclopedia of Life Kingdom: Animalia Mass: 160 kg (Adult) Encyclopedia of Life Order: Testudines Class: Reptiles Family: Cheloniidae

Anoa

The anoa or pygmy buffalo is an endemic buffalo that lives on the mainland of Sulawesi Island and Buton Island.
Many people refer to anoa as dwarf buffaloes. Anoa is an animal classified as a transitional fauna. Anoa are mammals belonging to the bovidae family that are spread almost throughout the island of Sulawesi.

Elang Flores

The Flores hawk (Nisaetus floris) is an endemic hawk species that inhabits the Lesser Sunda region with a distribution limited to the three islands of Lombok, Sumbawa and Flores and several other small islands including Komodo and Rinca.

Siamang

Siamang gibbons are black, long-armed apes that live in trees. In general, siamangs are very agile when moving in the trees, so no predator can catch them. The siamang is a threatened species, due to the rapid deforestation of its habitat. Mass: 11 kg (Female), 11 kg (Male) Encyclopedia of Life Gestation period: 231 days Encyclopedia of Life Kingdom: Animalia Family: Hylobatidae Class: Mammalia

Tarsius Kerdil

The pygmy krabuku or mountain Tangkasi, is a nocturnal primate found in Central Sulawesi, Indonesia, in areas of low vegetative species diversity rather than lowland tropical forest. The pygmy krabuku was believed to be extinct in the early 20th century. Length: 30 cm (female) Encyclopedia of Life Kingdom: Animalia Family: Tarsiidae Genus: Tarsius Class: Mammals

Burung Maleo

Maleo Senkawor or Maleo, whose scientific name is Macrocephalon maleo, is a medium-sized maleo bird, about 55 cm long, and the only bird in the single genus Macrocephalon. What is unique about the maleo is that the maleo chicks can already fly when they hatch.
